Your Personal Food Manager

▮Introduction

In this project I was the principle designer for our team, which created a food-management app that helps people reduce food waste. We envisioned an AI assistant that could suggest recipes and generate grocery lists by learning the user's food usage and monitoring the expiration state and quantity of the food in their fridge.

For the purpose of the class, the project ended up with an iOS application which only implemented the UI to allow users to interact with the App. The design process included:

  • Needfinding Interviews
  • Test Solutions
  • Concept Video
  • Low-fi Prototype
  • Medium-fi Prototype and Heuristic Evaluation
  • High-fi Prototype Implementation

▮ Needfinding Interviews Full Documentation on Brainstorm >>

From our section topic "home", we first narrowed down our problem domain to "new resident". We interviewed eight people, including both new home buyers and residents who have lived in the same house for many years. Our interview questions were designed to understand the interviewees' needs in renting, buying, maintaining and organizing a house. The interview results are summarized below.

"I just want my life in home to be as convenient as living in a dorm. "

Colin feels painful to sort dishes and clothes. He has never solved the problem of organizing the food stored in his hourse. He also usually forgets to buy healthy food like fruits and vegetable, and feel too lazy to go out after returning home.

"It's fun to live to other people. "

Alex and his roommates wished to spend more time outside, and they had been planning to set up a firepit in their small backyard. He and his wife already had a group of friends in the city, but they often wouldn't havethe time or energy to make plans for the weekends.

"We chose Sunnyvale because there are many indian restaurants, people and groceries there."

Neha and her husband have a house manager who takes care of the house. Neither of them like doing household chores, so they pay a cleaner to help clean their house once a month.

"It's hard transitioning from school to work life."

Peter lived with his friends (roommates) together but they rarely spend time together, even on his favorite computer games. He and his roommates were organized with their house roles despite that he hated laundry.

One important takeaway from our interview is that people are generally busy with work, and don't like to take care of the house themselves. They wish to spend more time outside with their friends and do something they like. We extracted three points of view from the insights and brainstormed many solutions for each of them. In the end, we chose to prototype three ideas to test their effectiveness.

▮ Prototypes Full Documentation on User Test >>

We prepared cards with drawn interfaces. As the testers "click" on some "buttons", we give them the card with the interface of the "next step". After walking through the app, we ask the testers for comments on it.

Prototype 1: Integrated App for Renting Process

The tester likes the app very much. It is surprising that she cares about appearance in selecting a housemate. We learned from the test that the match process should be fed more aspects of data, but not annoy the users.

Prototype 2: Outdoor Scene Generator

The tester really enjoyed the different settings for music generation. But he didn't know if the information such as generated games and activities would be useful. Also he thought the app too similar to Spotify mood generated playlists.

Prototype 3: Fridge with Computer Vision

The tester praised the incorporation of handheld device that made the idea more realistic for the scene. Yet she feels that the swipe-down on 2nd view to see inventory was not intuitive. She also pointed out recipes should be filterable by difficulty and cooking/prep time.

After testing our three solutions with prototypes, we decide to move on with the fidge with computer vision.

We then created a set of cards for pilot user tests. Testers need to finish three tasks in the test:

  • Navigate through the four main views via the menu.
  • Edit the grocery list using check boxes, adding button, and swiping deletion.
  • Discover Popups via unlabeled items in inventory and navigate to other windows through these popups.
